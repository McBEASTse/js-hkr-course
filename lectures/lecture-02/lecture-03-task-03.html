What we know about new Epstein emails that mention Trump

Summary

  • Document Release: US lawmakers released over 20,000 pages of documents from the Jeffrey Epstein estate.
  • Trump Mentioned: Some released emails mention President Donald Trump, leading to political dispute.
  • Partisan Conflict: Democrats released selected emails to raise questions, while Republicans countered with the full trove, calling the Democratic action a "smear" attempt.

US lawmakers have released over 20,000 pages of documents from the estate of the convicted sex offender Jeffrey Epstein , some of which mention President Donald Trump . Democrats on the House Oversight Committee initially released a few email exchanges, including some between Epstein and his associate Ghislaine Maxwell , as well as author Michael Wolff . In response, House Republicans quickly released a much larger set of documents, claiming the Democrats were "cherry-picking" and trying to " create a fake narrative to slander President Trump. " The White House echoed this, with Press Secretary Karoline Leavitt asserting the emails were "selectively leaked" to smear the president, and maintaining that Trump had severed ties with Epstein years ago over his inappropriate behavior, despite having been friends with him previously .
